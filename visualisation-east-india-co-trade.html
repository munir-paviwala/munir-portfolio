Abstract

The visualization above highlights the flow of different commodities across Asia by the British East India Company (EIC). It showcases the changes in trading patterns as the EIC transformed from a mercantile outfit into a colonial force in South Asia.

Reflections on the Data

Despite the monumental work of K. N. Chaudhari and H. V. Bowen, the limitations of the data are shocking. For the period of 1660 to 1760, we don't know where exports from London went. This comes up in the visualisation when you try to filter the data based on location.

Data Sources

  • 1660–1760: K. N. Chaudhari, The Trading World of Asia... (1976).
  • 1760–1834: H. V. Bowen, The Business of Empire... (2005).

Limitations

Chaudhari's data lacks destination details for exports. Bowen's data provides origin/destination points. Merging these datasets reveals gaps, particularly when filtering for specific ports prior to 1760.
